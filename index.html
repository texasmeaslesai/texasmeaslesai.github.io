07

But There's a Catch

RL is effective but inefficient.

Total Vaccines Used

While RL reduced infections slightly more than High-Risk targeting, it consumed far more vaccines to achieve that result. High-Risk achieved 84.6% vaccine efficiency compared to RL's 4.5%.

This raises an important question: Is the marginal improvement worth the additional resource cost?

08

Stress Testing the AI

We analyzed robustness of the RL model with three additional experiments.

  • 01

    Vaccine Hesitancy

    Tested at 0%, 20%, 30%, and 50% hesitancy. RL remained best across all levels, but higher hesitancy created a tipping point where RL's advantage diminished and it became similar to High-Risk targeting.

  • 02

    Total Vaccine Budget

    RL performed poorly with limited budgets. At 200k vaccines, RL's peak infections (4,383) were actually worse than High-Risk (677). Larger budgets showed diminishing returns for RL.

  • 03

    Daily Vaccine Supply

    Tested at 0, 2,500, 5,000, and 10,000 doses per day. RL maintained the lowest infections across all supply levels, but high supply rates led to diminishing returns with High-Risk achieving similar effectiveness.

The Answer

Maybe
Could AI Have Saved Texas?

AI can significantly reduce measles spread, even with substantial limitations, but it's not a silver bullet.

Key Takeaways

‚úì RL does significantly reduce measles spread, even under challenging conditions

‚úì RL tends to achieve the lowest peak infections across scenarios

‚úó RL uses vaccines inefficiently compared to High-Risk targeting (4.5% vs 84.6% efficiency)

‚úó With limited total budgets, RL actually performs worse than simpler strategies

‚ö†Ô∏è High-Risk targeting often achieves nearly equivalent results with far less complexity

The true answer depends on priorities and limitations.

If minimizing peak infections is paramount and resources are abundant, RL performs best. However, if you're working with limited vaccines or care about vaccine efficiency, a heuristic like High-Risk targeting may be the better choice.

When hospital capacity is the limiting factor, RL's peak suppression approach is preferable despite lower vaccine efficiency. The computational cost and training requirements may limit real-time deployment during actual outbreaks.

AI is not a catch-all solution. It depends on the priorities of the vaccination program and the limitations at play.

This analysis is based on multi-county SEIR modeling and reinforcement learning simulations fitted to the 2025 Texas measles outbreak data. Model parameters were fitted using Poisson deviance minimization via the Nelder-Mead algorithm (Pseudo-R¬≤ = 0.659, RMSE = 2.71 cases/day). The RL agent uses Q-learning with Œµ-greedy exploration, training on outbreak dynamics over 180 days.
